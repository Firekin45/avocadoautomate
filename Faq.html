Where is my refund?
Call 1‑800‑829‑1040 or use the IRS “Where’s My Refund?” tool. IRS usually issues 90%+ of refunds within 21 days.
How long does it take to get my refund?
Most refunds arrive within 21 days, though some may take longer depending on IRS review.
What forms do I need to file my taxes?
Any mail marked "IMPORTANT TAX INFORMATION ENCLOSED" — W‑2s, 1099s, and dependent info (names, SSNs, DOBs), etc.
What is the IRS refund schedule?
  • E‑file + direct deposit: 1–3 weeks
  • E‑file + check: ~1 month
  • Mail + direct deposit: ~3 weeks
  • Mail + check: ~2 months
What happens after I e-file?
We submit your return to the IRS. They review it for 24–48 hours. Once accepted, your refund is processed according to the IRS timeline.
What does it mean when my tax return is accepted?
It means the IRS verified your identity and initial checks are complete. The next step is refund approval and issuance.
Is it faster to e-file or mail my return?
E-filing with direct deposit is the fastest method.
What tax breaks delay my refund?
Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC) and Additional Child Tax Credit often delay refunds until early March.
Should I file early?
Yes! It reduces stress and gets your refund faster. Plus, if you owe, you have more time to plan.
What if I miss the deadline?
You can request an extension until Oct 15 to file, but payment is still due by April 15 to avoid penalties.
Are there key tax deadlines?
Yes: April 15 (filing/payment deadline), October 15 (extension deadline).
Can you go to jail for back taxes?
Not for owing taxes. But fraudulent activity or willful evasion could result in penalties or legal issues.
How many years can I file back taxes?
Up to six years from the end of the relevant assessment year.
Will I get a stimulus check if I haven’t filed in 5 years?
No. You must have filed or used the IRS Non-Filer tool to qualify.
